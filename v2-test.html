Model Differences

Component V1 (Current) V2 (Proposed) Rationale
Scaling Function Logarithmic: log(1+x)/log(1+ceiling) Square root: √(x/ceiling) Log compresses the top end too aggressively — routes with 130 and 260 ft/mi gain both hit the ceiling. Sqrt still expands the low end but preserves meaningful separation at the top. A route with 210 ft/mi now scores 89, not 100.
Climb Density 20% weight, ceiling 160 ft/mi 40% weight, ceiling 264 ft/mi Gain/mile is the most intuitive, universal metric. Was underweighted. Higher ceiling allows the harder routes more headroom.
Gradient Intensity 35% weight, gradient^2.0, ceiling 20 35% weight, gradient^1.5, ceiling 8 Keep weight, soften exponent. 2.0 over-penalizes moderate grades. Lower ceiling with sqrt scaling produces equivalent mid-range scores.
Steep Proportion 45% weight, binary >8% cutoff Removed Binary threshold creates cliff-edge scoring (7.9% = 0, 8.1% = 100). Highly correlated with Intensity, which already captures steepness on a continuum.
Climb Continuity n/a 25% weight (new), ceiling 1500m Sustained climbs feel much hillier than many small bumps with same total gain. Uses distance-weighted average climb length blended with longest single climb (60/40).
